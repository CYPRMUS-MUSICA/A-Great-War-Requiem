Chapter Five

Postlude to a Great War Requiem

Conclusion

Throughout the composition of A Great War Requiem, I have endeavored to bring awareness to a significant oversight of women’s Great War poetry. The purpose of this study was to create a War Requiem that brings awareness to female narratives of the Great War by using Great War Poetry by women poets, specifically the compelling works of Vera Brittain, Winifred M. Letts, Margaret Cole, and Josephine Preston Peabody into an elegiac work—A Great War Requiem. The integration of female Great War Poetry and the reimagining of Requiem Mass plainchant converges to make a unique composition firmly rooted in the Requiem Mass musical composition tradition and the War Requiem tradition. A Great War Requiem underscores the profound and nuanced perspectives that women poets brought to bear upon the traumatic events of the Great War.

The central focus of the dissertation, the musical composition, demonstrates how ancient Requiem Mass plainchant melodies can be creatively reimagined for contemporary audiences, thereby becoming powerful conduits for conveying the complex emotional landscapes of remembrance, war, and loss. In addition, this work has illuminated the varied ways in which the women poets Brittain, Letts, Cole, and Preston-Peabody grappled with the war’s profound and far-reaching impact. Brittain, Letts, Cole, and Peabody’s works showcase women’s varied experiences during the Great War. The poets highlight the significant role that women played in shaping the historical perspectives of the conflict. A Great War Requiem combines women’s Great War poetry, Requiem Mass texts, Requiem Mass plainchant, and modern music to convey themes such as rest, loss, death, and humanity’s desire for peace.

Future Plans for A Great War Requiem

In addition to the SATB Choir and organ version of A Great War Requiem, the dissertation’s I intend to create several composition arrangements, including wind band arrangements, brass band versions, an orchestral version, and an edition for Choir and orchestra. The author wants to introduce A Great War Requiem to various performing venues. Re-orchestrating it for choir and orchestra would make it more accessible to different audiences and highlight the female narratives of the Great War.
