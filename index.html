Requiem aeternam et Rest
A significant shortcoming in the scholarship of Great War poetry lies in the lack of attention paid to women's contributions. — Kaylene Cypret, Introit

A Great War Requiem is a researched music composition that pairs remembrance poetry from the Great War with the Requiem Mass tradition. The purpose of this study is to create a War Requiem composition that brings awareness to female narratives of the Great War through poetry by women poets. The dissertation composition combines Requiem aeternam, Lux aeterna, and Requiescant in pace texts from the Requiem Mass with Vera Brittain's "Roundel," Winifred Mabel Letts's "Your Name," Margaret Cole's "Rest" and "Falling Leaves," and Josephine Preston Peabody's "Harvest Moon."

The seven-movement composition is approximately forty-five minutes long and scored for mixed SATB choir and organ. By integrating these previously marginalized perspectives, the work broadens the understanding of the impact of the Great War and offers a more nuanced account of mourning, rest, and remembrance — a sonic memorial to the women who wrote, waited, nursed, and grieved.

In memory of Tammy Tallacksen Cypret and Wendall Cypret.
